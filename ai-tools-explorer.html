Google NotebookLM

notebook.lm

"Your sources, your podcast, your brain — grounded."

Google's AI research notebook powered by Gemini. Upload PDFs, Docs, Slides, websites, YouTube, and audio — then chat with citations, generate study guides, and spin up Audio Overviews (AI "Deep Dive" podcasts) from your material.

Best for

  • Students & researchers digesting dense reading
  • Teams turning docs into briefings & FAQs
  • Learning on the go via Audio Overviews
  • Source-grounded Q&A (fewer hallucinations)

Pricing (approx.)

Free$0 — ~100 notebooks, 50 sources each, 3 audio/day
Plusvia Google AI Premium ~$20/mo
Pro / UltraHigher limits; Ultra ~$250/mo enterprise-scale

II-Agent

agent.ii

"Open-source agent that actually ships — BYOK optional."

Matched: agent.ii → II-Agent by Intelligent Internet (agent.ii.inc / ii.inc). V1 launched Feb 2026; Apache-2.0 on GitHub. Not Agent.ai (no-code marketplace).

Production-ready autonomous agent: full-stack apps, deep research, slides & storybooks, browser automation, doc editing (PDF/Excel/PPT), and integrations (Gmail, Slack, GitHub, Notion). Multi-model threads (Gemini, Claude, GPT) with Plan mode and custom skills from GitHub repos.

Best for

  • Developers who want self-host + Docker control
  • Power users mixing models in one conversation
  • Research → deliverable workflows (reports, decks)
  • Teams automating workflows via custom skills

Pricing (approx.)

Open source$0 — self-host; you pay API usage
Hosted web appFree tier / waitlist; BYOK = your API keys
Your keysPay providers directly (usage varies)

Replit Agent

replit.com

"Describe the app. Watch it build, test, and deploy."

Cloud IDE meets AI builder. Replit Agent plans and ships web & mobile apps, dashboards, designs, and slides — with Lite/Economy/Power build modes, Design Canvas, connectors (Stripe, OpenAI, etc.), and effort-based billing so simple tasks stay cheap.

Best for

  • Non-devs prototyping apps fast
  • Devs who want deploy + collab in one place
  • MVPs with integrations out of the box
  • Teams pooling monthly AI credits (Pro)

Pricing (approx.)

Starter$0 — daily Agent cap, Lite builds
Core~$20/mo — full Agent, 5 collaborators
Pro~$100/mo — Turbo Mode, $100+ credits, 15 builders
Agent usageEffort-based; many tasks < $0.25

Suno

suno.com

"Type a vibe. Get a banger. (Maybe even commercial rights.)"

Leading AI music generator — full songs from text prompts with v4.5–v5.5 models, stem separation, advanced editing on paid tiers, and Suno Studio on Premier. Credit-based: ~5 credits per generation; plans scale to thousands of songs/month.

Best for

  • Creators prototyping tracks & jingles
  • Content makers needing royalty-ready audio (Pro+)
  • Quick iteration with priority queues
  • Exploring styles before hitting the studio

Pricing (approx.)

Free$0 — ~50 credits/day (~10 songs)
Pro~$8/mo — 2,500 credits, commercial use
Premier~$24/mo — 10,000 credits + Studio

Udio

udo → Udio

"Suno's rival in the ring — stems, remixes, major-label vibes."

Clarified: udo is almost certainly Udio (udio.com) — AI music, not a typo of "udo" the language.

AI music creation with strong audio quality, Style & Remix tools on Pro, stem downloads, and a licensing push in 2026 (UMG, Warner, Merlin). Great for producers who want more control per track than a one-shot prompt.

Best for

  • Producers wanting stems & remix workflows
  • Budget-conscious creators vs. premium rivals
  • Commercial projects (paid tiers)
  • Experimenting before licensed-catalog pricing shifts

Pricing (approx.)

Free$0 — daily + monthly credits, non-commercial
Standard~$10/mo — 2,400 credits, commercial
Pro~$30/mo — 6,000 credits, highest quality

Kling AI

klingai.com

"Hollywood dreams, credit wallet reality — text to cinematic clip."

Kuaishou's AI video powerhouse: text-to-video & image-to-video up to ~2 min (extendable), 1080p, native audio/lip-sync (2.6+), physics-aware motion, and character consistency. Credit-based tiers; intro promos often renew higher.

Best for

  • Short-form video & social content
  • Marketing clips with synced dialogue
  • Storyboards → motion proofs
  • Creators who outgrew slideshow AI

Pricing (approx.)

Free$0 — limited monthly credits
Standard~$7–9/mo intro (660 credits)
Pro / Premier~$26–65/mo (3k–8k credits)
Ultra~$120–180/mo heavy creators

FLORA + FAUNA

flora fauna

"Fifty models, one canvas — FAUNA runs the creative orchestra."

Matched: flora fauna → FLORA (flora.ai) creative environment + FAUNA, its in-canvas AI agent (launched Mar 2026). Brand domain: florafauna.ai.

Professional node-based canvas wiring 50+ image, video, and text models. FAUNA picks models, sequences workflows, researches references (incl. Unsplash), and shows every step — built for design teams (Nike, Netflix-tier users), not one-click hobbyists.

Best for

  • Design teams iterating campaigns visually
  • Art directors chaining multi-model pipelines
  • Agencies billing exact per-generation costs
  • FAUNA-assisted research → production flows

Pricing (approx., new model May 2026)

Free$0 — explore text/image; FAUNA free
Starter~$18/seat/mo — $18 usage budget
Pro / Max~$50–200/seat/mo + launch bonuses
FAUNAFree on all plans — doesn't eat budget

Cockpit

kriya

"Workers that finish the work — not chatbots that type about it."

Matched: kriya → kriya.ai now brands as Cockpit (oncockpit.ai) — autonomous AI workers for sales (prospecting, outreach, CRM, meetings). Older "Kriya AI" listings described email-intro networking; the live product is the Cockpit OS.

"Operating system for autonomous work": AI workers read inbox, calendar, contacts, and docs natively — run parallel outreach, remember context across sessions, and hand off work. Sales workflows ship now; Worker Studio for custom ops/marketing/HR lands fall 2026.

Best for

  • Sales teams scaling outbound without more SDRs
  • Founders who want meetings booked, not drafts
  • Ops leaders tired of copy-paste between tools
  • Orgs ready for expert onboarding + human-in-the-loop

Pricing (approx.)
